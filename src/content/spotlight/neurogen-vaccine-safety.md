AsPEN's contribution to vaccine-safety monitoring grew out of its long-running tradition of cataloguing and characterising the population-level health databases available across the Asia-Pacific. When the COVID-19 vaccines arrived, the network was already positioned to ask a practical question: which of the region's databases could realistically support a coordinated, multi-country vaccine-safety assessment?

The 2021 paper in *Pharmacoepidemiology and Drug Safety* led by Lai EC and colleagues addressed exactly that. Drawing on AsPEN's existing inventory of national claims, electronic health records, and registries, the team systematically evaluated 19 candidate databases across the Asia-Pacific against a set of criteria for collaborative vaccine-safety monitoring: data lag, linkage to immunisation records, capture of relevant adverse events, and the institutional pathways for shared analysis. The result was a concrete map of which sites could feasibly contribute to a multi-country signal-detection effort, and which gaps the network would need to work around.

That database-evaluation work was the seed for the larger infrastructure described in the 2023 *Clinical Epidemiology* paper by Tsai DH and colleagues. NeuroGEN, the Cross-Regional Data Initiative for the Assessment and Development of Treatment for Neurological Diseases, now spans 15 databases across Asia, Oceania, Europe, and North America. The platform pairs the AsPEN approach to harmonised protocols and site-level analysis with the broader NeuroGEN partner sites in the UK (CPRD, THIN), Scotland (Public Health Scotland), the Netherlands (PHARMO), Finland (FinReg), Australia (PBS, VLHD), and the US (Medicare, Medicaid). The same evaluation framework, applied at scale, lets NeuroGEN execute the kind of coordinated multi-country observational study that vaccine-safety surveillance, comparative drug-utilisation research, and rare-event signal detection all rely on.

Together the two papers trace the line from a single regional vaccine-safety question to a standing four-continent platform that AsPEN, NeuroGEN, and their partner sites now use for routine collaborative research.
